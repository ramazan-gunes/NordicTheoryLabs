# Driving licence cost in Sweden: where the money goes

The cost of getting a category B driving licence in Sweden can vary a lot. Two learners may pay very different totals, even if they pass the same tests. The reason is simple: some costs are fixed official fees, while the largest part usually depends on how much training you need.

This guide explains the main cost items so you can plan with less surprise. Fees and prices can change, so always check Trafikverket, Transportstyrelsen, your optician and your driving school before you budget.

## Why this topic matters

Many people ask for one total price. That number is rarely useful. A better approach is to understand the cost categories: official tests, licence production, eye test, risk training, theory material, driving lessons and possible retakes.

Once you see the categories, you can control the parts that are flexible. You cannot negotiate Trafikverket’s theory test fee, but you can reduce wasted lessons by practising consistently and studying theory early.

## How costs work in Sweden

Trafikverket charges for the theory test, driving test, test photography and, if needed, car hire for the driving test. Transportstyrelsen charges the production fee when your licence is issued. Other costs are market prices: opticians, driving schools, risk training providers and study tools.

The official fees are only a small part of many learners’ total cost. Lessons and repeated tests can become the expensive part.

## Step-by-step cost items

### Learner’s permit and eye test

The learner’s permit application itself is usually not the big cost, but the eye test is paid to the provider who performs it. Prices vary between opticians and locations.

If you need medical certificates or additional checks, the cost can be higher. Most category B learners only need a standard eye test.

### Introductory course for private practice

If you want to practise privately with a supervisor, you and the supervisor must complete the introductory course. This is paid to the course provider. Private practice can save money later, but the setup still has a cost.

### Theory study

Theory materials can be a book, an app, online questions or a course. This part is often small compared with driving lessons, but poor theory preparation can become expensive if it leads to retakes or delayed practical progress.

### Riskettan and risktvaan

Risk education is compulsory for category B. Riskettan and risktvaan are paid to approved training providers, and prices vary. Risktvaan often costs more because it requires a facility and practical exercises.

Do not leave these courses to the last minute. Late booking can limit your options.

### Driving lessons

Driving lessons are usually the largest variable cost. The number you need depends on previous experience, how often you practise privately, how quickly you build safe traffic judgement and how well you understand Swedish test expectations.

An experienced driver may need fewer lessons but still need coaching on Swedish habits. A beginner may need many more hours. The cheapest plan is not always the fewest lessons; it is the plan with the least wasted practice.

### Theory test, photo and driving test

Trafikverket lists the category B theory test at SEK 420 during daytime and SEK 520 after 6 pm or at weekends. The category B driving test is listed at SEK 1000 during daytime and SEK 1400 after 6 pm or at weekends. Photography when taking a test is listed at SEK 120. If you use Trafikverket’s car hire for category B, that is listed at SEK 800.

These official prices were checked on 21 May 2026, but you should confirm them before booking.

### Licence production

Transportstyrelsen charges a production fee for the driving licence. Its English information page lists SEK 375 for producing a new licence. Confirm the current fee before paying.

## Common hidden costs

Retakes are the first hidden cost. A failed theory test or driving test is not only another fee. It can also mean more lessons, more travel and more waiting.

Travel is another cost. If the risk course, driving school or test centre is far away, public transport, fuel or time off work matters.

Changing plans can also cost money. A learner who starts with only private practice and later needs many school lessons may pay more than expected. A learner who takes school lessons but never practises between them may also spend too much.

## Newcomers and immigrants

If you already drive, budget for adaptation rather than only basic driving. Swedish traffic culture may require more observation, speed discipline and cyclist awareness than you expect.

If Swedish is not your strongest language, add time and possibly tools for vocabulary. Theory can be studied in another language, but test-day logistics and driving instructions still require basic Swedish road words.

## Common mistakes

The biggest budgeting mistake is counting only official fees. For most learners, lessons and training are the main cost.

Another mistake is buying too many tools but not using them consistently. One good theory system used every day is better than five subscriptions opened once.

A third mistake is booking tests before being ready. Retakes make the process more expensive and more stressful.

## Short summary

Your total cost depends mostly on training needs, not only official fees. Plan for eye test, private practice setup if needed, theory tools, risk courses, driving lessons, Trafikverket tests, photo, possible car hire, licence production and a buffer for retakes.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is the biggest cost?

Usually driving lessons, because the number of lessons varies widely.

### Are official test fees the whole cost?

No. Official fees are only part of the total. Training and retakes often matter more.

### Can private practice reduce the cost?

Yes, if the supervisor is approved and the practice is structured. Poor private practice can also create habits that later need paid correction.

### Where should I check current prices?

Use Trafikverket for test and photo fees, Transportstyrelsen for licence production fees and your local providers for training prices.
