# Learner's permit and eye test in Sweden: the first step to a B licence

Before you can practise driving for a category B licence in Sweden, you need a valid **learner's permit**. In Swedish, this is called **körkortstillstånd**. It is not the same as booking a driving lesson or starting a theory app. It is the official permission from Transportstyrelsen that allows you to begin practising.

If you skip this step, the rest of the process becomes fragile. Driving schools, private supervisors and test planning all depend on having the permit in place.

## What the application includes

The learner's permit application normally includes a health declaration and an eye test certificate. The health declaration helps Transportstyrelsen assess whether there is anything medical that affects driving safety. The eye test certificate, called **synintyg**, confirms that your eyesight meets the requirements or that you need correction such as glasses or lenses.

The eye test is usually done by an optician or another authorised provider. The provider can often send the result digitally, but always ask how they report it before you leave.

## When can you start practising?

For passenger car category B, you can start practising at **16 years old**, as long as you have a valid learner's permit. The age for taking the licence itself is higher, but practising can begin earlier.

Do not confuse permission to practise with being ready for the test. The permit opens the door. You still need training, risk courses, theory preparation and the Trafikverket tests.

## If you practise privately

Private driving practice has extra requirements. You need an approved accompanying driver, called a **handledare**. For passenger car practice, both learner and supervisor must complete a three-hour introduction course before practising together.

The supervisor is responsible during private practice. That means private driving should be planned seriously: calm routes first, clear goals and no pressure to practise in traffic that is too difficult too early.

## Common mistakes

The first mistake is booking lessons or asking someone to supervise before the permit is approved. Start the paperwork early so you do not lose weeks.

The second mistake is treating the eye test as a small formality. If you need glasses or lenses, use them consistently while practising. Your training should match how you will drive on test day.

The third mistake is forgetting that private practice and driving school practice are not identical. Private practice requires the approved supervisor setup. A driving school handles supervision through its authorised instructors.

## Simple checklist

Apply for the learner's permit, complete the health declaration, take the eye test, wait for approval and then plan your practice. If you will practise privately, make sure the supervisor is approved and the introduction course requirement is complete.

Once this first step is stable, the rest of the Swedish licence process becomes much easier to organise.
