# Common mistakes on the Swedish theory test

The Swedish theory test is not only a memory test. It checks whether you understand traffic rules, risks and safe decisions. Many learners fail because they study the wrong way.

## Memorising practice questions

Practice questions are useful, but they are not the real test. If you only memorise answers, a differently worded question can become difficult.

After each wrong answer, ask why the correct answer is safest and most rule-consistent.

## Ignoring risk awareness

Many questions are about judgement: speed, margins, visibility, fatigue, alcohol, cyclists and pedestrians. The safest answer is often the one that reduces risk, even if it is slower.

## Reading too quickly

Small words matter. Questions may include "first", "most important", "not" or "except". Read slowly before choosing.

## Language problems

If you take the test in another language or in Swedish as a second language, build a vocabulary list. Learn words for road users, weather, priority, obligation and prohibition.

## Studying without real traffic logic

Connect every rule to a real situation. That makes the answer easier to remember and easier to apply later in the driving test.
