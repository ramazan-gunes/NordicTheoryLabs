# Swedish driving test for category B: what happens during körprov?

The Swedish driving test is called **körprov**. It is the practical Trafikverket test for a category B licence and shows whether you can drive safely, independently and with good judgement in real traffic.

Passing the theory test is not enough. During the driving test, you need to show that you can use your knowledge while the road, traffic and conditions keep changing.

## Before the test starts

Bring valid identification and arrive early. If Trafikverket provides the test vehicle, make sure you understand the basic controls before the drive begins. If you use another approved vehicle, check the rules carefully before test day.

The test is conducted in Swedish. Trafikverket provides driving test information in several languages before the test, but the examiner's instructions during the drive are normally in Swedish. Practise the common instruction words early: turn, continue, follow signs, park, reverse and stop.

## The safety check

The driving test can include a safety check. You may be asked to check something inside or outside the car, such as lights, brakes, tyres, steering, windscreen wipers or visibility.

The examiner is not expecting a mechanic. The point is that you understand whether the car is safe to drive and can explain or show a basic check calmly.

## Manoeuvres

You may need to perform special manoeuvres. This can include parking, reversing, turning around or positioning the car accurately in a limited space.

The examiner looks at control, observation and judgement. A small correction is usually less serious than rushing, missing pedestrians or failing to look around. Move slowly, use mirrors and blind-spot checks, and show that you know what is happening around the car.

## Driving in traffic

The largest part of the test is driving in real traffic. You may drive in urban areas, on larger roads, in roundabouts, near pedestrians and cyclists, and on roads with different speed limits.

You need to show that you can adapt speed, keep safe margins, plan ahead, follow signs and make independent decisions. The examiner wants to see a driver who is safe and predictable, not someone who only follows instructions mechanically.

## Automatic or manual car

If you take the test in a car with automatic transmission, your licence will normally be restricted to automatic vehicles. In Sweden this is marked with condition **78**. If you later want to drive manual cars, you need to take another driving test in a manual car.

This is an important planning choice. Automatic can be a good option for some learners, but make the decision knowingly.

## Common reasons learners fail

Many failed tests are not about one dramatic mistake. They are often about repeated weak decisions: driving too fast for the situation, poor observation, late lane changes, unsafe gaps, weak planning or not giving enough attention to vulnerable road users.

Another common issue is passive driving. You should not wait for the examiner to solve traffic for you. You are expected to read the situation and make safe decisions yourself.

## How to prepare

Practise in varied traffic, not only on familiar streets. Work on roundabouts, lane changes, parking, country roads, city traffic, speed adaptation and interaction with cyclists and pedestrians.

Before test day, ask your instructor or supervisor to give you mock-test drives where they speak less and let you drive more independently. The goal is not perfect driving. The goal is safe, calm and responsible driving even when the situation is new.
