# Getting a driving licence in Sweden: the practical guide for category B

Getting a Swedish driving licence can feel confusing at first, especially if you are new to the country or do not study comfortably in Swedish. The process is not just one test. It is a chain of permits, training, risk education, theory knowledge and practical driving ability.

A clear step-by-step guide to the Swedish category B driving licence process, from learner's permit and risk courses to the theory and driving tests.. Use it to connect theory, practice and safe decisions before the Swedish driving test.

Rules, fees and booking availability can change, so always confirm details with Transportstyrelsen and Trafikverket before you book anything.

## How the Swedish driving licence process works

Two authorities are important. Transportstyrelsen, the Swedish Transport Agency, handles driving licence permits, medical requirements, supervision for private practice and the final licence. Trafikverket, the Swedish Transport Administration, runs the theory test and the practical driving test.

In a typical order, you apply for a learner's permit, complete an eye test, start theory and driving practice, complete risk education parts 1 and 2, take the theory test and then take the driving test.

## 1. Apply for körkortstillstånd

Körkortstillstånd is the learner's permit. You need it before you are allowed to practise driving. The application is made through Transportstyrelsen and includes a health declaration. You also need an eye test certificate.

If you plan to practise privately, your supervisor must be approved. For category B, you and the private supervisor must also complete an introductory course before private practice starts. If you only drive with an authorised driving school, the school manages the training environment, but you still need the learner's permit.

## 2. Take the eye test

The eye test, synundersökning, checks that your eyesight meets the requirements for driving. It is usually done at an optician or another approved provider. The result is sent to Transportstyrelsen or attached according to the current application flow.

Do this early. Without the permit and eye certificate in order, the rest of the process can stall.

## 3. Study theory while you practise driving

In Sweden, theory is not separate from practical driving. The theory test covers road rules, signs, vehicle knowledge, environment, safety and driver behaviour. The driving test checks whether you can apply that knowledge in real traffic.

A common mistake is to memorise questions instead of understanding the system. Trafikverket is clear that practice questions are not the same as the official test. Learn why a rule exists, how signs relate to road situations and how risk changes with speed, weather, fatigue and other road users.

## 4. Complete riskettan

Riskettan is the first part of the compulsory risk education. It focuses on alcohol, drugs, fatigue and risky behaviour. It is usually classroom-based and helps you understand how decisions before and during driving affect safety.

Do not treat it as a formality. The topics appear indirectly in both theory and practical driving: judgement, self-control, margins and responsibility.

## 5. Complete risktvaan

Risktvaan is the second part of the risk course. It focuses on speed, safety, road conditions and difficult driving situations. Many learners know it as the skid training, but the important point is not entertainment or performance. It is learning how little control you have when speed, surface and reaction time work against you.

For category B, both risk courses must be completed and valid when you take the tests. A completed risk course is normally valid for five years, but check your own status with Transportstyrelsen.

## 6. Take the theory test

The category B theory test is taken at Trafikverket. You need valid identification. You are photographed before the first theory test, so arrive early.

The test has 70 multiple-choice questions. Five are sample questions that do not count, so the maximum score is 65. You have 50 minutes, and you need 52 correct answers to pass. If you pass, the result is valid for four months. You must pass the theory test before taking the driving test.

The test can be booked in other languages for category B, and people with reading difficulties or other needs may be able to apply for support such as extended time, an oral test or an interpreter. Check the official requirements before booking.

## 7. Take the driving test

The driving test is where you show that you can drive safely, independently and with good judgement. The examiner may assess a safety check, manoeuvres and driving in traffic. You may drive in urban and rural environments depending on the test route.

The driving test itself is not booked in another language. Trafikverket states that the examiner speaks Swedish, but information videos may be available in other languages before the test. Practise the Swedish words you may hear: höger, vänster, parkera, vänd, motorväg, landsväg and so on.

If you take the driving test in an automatic car, your licence may receive condition 78, which means you may only drive automatic cars. To drive manual later, you need a new practical test in a manual car.

## Driving school or private practice?

A driving school gives structure, professional feedback and access to a training car. It is especially useful for learners who already drive but have habits that do not match Swedish test expectations.

Private practice can reduce cost and add many driving hours, but it depends on a calm, approved supervisor who understands Swedish rules. The best route for many people is a mix: use a school to diagnose weaknesses and private practice to repeat skills between lessons.

## How long and how much does it cost?

The time and cost vary a lot. Your previous experience, Swedish language level, how often you practise, test availability and the number of professional lessons all matter. Fees for tests and licences can change, and driving schools set their own prices.

Think of the budget in parts: learner's permit and eye test, introductory course if practising privately, lessons, riskettan, risktvaan, theory material, theory test, driving test and car rental for the test if needed.

## Tips if Swedish is not your first language

Learn the core Swedish driving words even if you study theory in another language. The road is Swedish: signs, road markings, place names and examiner instructions use Swedish. Build a small glossary and practise with it during lessons.

Study in your strongest language first, then review the Swedish terms. This helps you understand the idea without guessing, while still preparing you for real traffic.

## Common mistakes

Learners often book tests too early, study only mock questions, ignore environmental driving, forget safety checks, drive too passively or fail to observe cyclists and pedestrians properly. Another common problem is treating the theory test as a memory game instead of a safety test.

## Short summary

Get your learner's permit, complete the eye test, practise theory and driving together, finish riskettan and risktvaan, pass the theory test and then pass the driving test. Start early, verify official details and build understanding instead of memorising answers.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need körkortstillstånd before practising?

Yes. You need a valid learner's permit before you can learn to drive.

### Is the risk course compulsory?

Yes, for category B you must complete both parts before the tests, and they must still be valid.

### Can I take the theory test in another language?

For category B, Trafikverket offers theory tests in other languages and support options may be available. Check the current booking rules.

### Can I take the driving test in English?

The driving test is not booked in another language. The examiner speaks Swedish, so learn basic driving instructions.

### Where should I verify current rules?

Use Transportstyrelsen for permits and licence rules, and Trafikverket for theory and driving test details.
