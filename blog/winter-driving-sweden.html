Winter driving in Sweden: what learners should practise

Swedish winter driving changes everything.

Snow, ice, darkness and wet roads make normal mistakes more serious.

Learners should practise smooth steering, longer safety margins, gentle braking and early planning.

Winter tyres are required in winter road conditions during the official winter period, and current tyre rules should be checked with Transportstyrelsen.

Do not treat winter driving as a separate trick.

It is ordinary safe driving with more patience, more distance and fewer sudden movements.

Practise starts, stops, turns, hill starts and parking in safe places before entering busy traffic.

On test day and in daily driving, the examiner expects speed adaptation and risk awareness, not bravery.

Practical quality checklist

This page is most useful when you connect it to real driving practice. For this topic, keep the main focus clear: Winter driving in Sweden is about tyres, visibility, distance, low speed and calm decisions on slippery roads.

  • Before you start, name the exact risk you are training today and where it usually appears in Swedish traffic.
  • During practice, connect the theory rule to observation, speed, road position and communication with other road users.
  • After practice, write down one situation that felt late or uncertain and decide what earlier clue you should notice next time.
  • When reviewing practice questions, explain why the safe answer is safe instead of only remembering the correct option.

Mini practice scenario

Choose a normal route near home, a driving school or Trafikverket-style test area. Drive it once slowly with the goal of noticing Winter driving in Sweden is about tyres, visibility, distance, low speed and calm decisions on slippery roads. Then repeat the same route and ask whether your decisions became earlier, calmer and easier to explain.

Official check
