Common mistakes on the Swedish driving test

The Swedish driving test is not about perfect driving. It is about safe, responsible and independent driving in real traffic. Many failed tests are caused by repeated weak decisions rather than one dramatic error.

Weak observation

Mirrors are not enough. You need to show that you check blind spots, cyclists, pedestrians, crossings and traffic behind you before changing position.

Poor speed adaptation

Driving at the speed limit is not always safe. You must adapt to visibility, weather, road surface, children, cyclists and complex junctions.

Late planning

Late lane changes, sudden braking and rushed decisions show weak planning. Look further ahead and prepare earlier.

Unsafe gaps

At junctions and roundabouts, do not enter just because there is space. The gap must be safe for you and for other road users.

Passive driving

The examiner expects you to make decisions. Waiting for instructions in every situation can look unsafe.

How to train

Practise in varied traffic and ask your instructor to give less help during mock tests. Your goal is calm, predictable driving with good margins.

Practical quality checklist

This page is most useful when you connect it to real driving practice. For this topic, keep the main focus clear: Common körprov mistakes in Sweden: weak observation, speed adaptation, late planning, unsafe gaps, passive driving and poor attention to vulnerable road users.

  • Before you start, name the exact risk you are training today and where it usually appears in Swedish traffic.
  • During practice, connect the theory rule to observation, speed, road position and communication with other road users.
  • After practice, write down one situation that felt late or uncertain and decide what earlier clue you should notice next time.
  • When reviewing practice questions, explain why the safe answer is safe instead of only remembering the correct option.

Mini practice scenario

Choose a normal route near home, a driving school or Trafikverket-style test area. Drive it once slowly with the goal of noticing Common körprov mistakes in Sweden: weak observation, speed adaptation, late planning, unsafe gaps, passive driving and poor attention to vulnerable road users. Then repeat the same route and ask whether your decisions became earlier, calmer and easier to explain.
