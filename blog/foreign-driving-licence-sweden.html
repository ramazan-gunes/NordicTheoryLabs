Foreign Driving Licence in Sweden: Can You Drive or Exchange It?

A practical guide for newcomers with a foreign driving licence: EEA licences, non-EEA licences, the one-year rule, translations and exchange options.

May 22, 2026 6 min Nordic Theory Labs

Foreign driving licence in Sweden: can you drive or exchange it?

If you move to Sweden with a driving licence from another country, the first question is usually simple: can you keep driving? The answer depends on where the licence was issued, whether it is still valid and whether you are registered as living in Sweden.

This guide gives a practical overview. Always check the current rule with Transportstyrelsen before you rely on your licence, because exceptions and document requirements matter.

If your licence is from the EEA

A driving licence from another EEA country is normally valid in Sweden as long as it is valid in the country where it was issued and has not been exchanged for a Swedish licence.

If you become permanently resident in Sweden, you may usually apply to exchange a valid EEA licence for a Swedish licence. This can be useful if you want Swedish documents, but it is not always urgent if the EEA licence remains valid.

If your licence is from outside the EEA

For a non-EEA driving licence to be valid in Sweden, it must normally be valid in the issuing country and it must not have been exchanged for a Swedish licence. The major planning rule is the one-year rule: a non-EEA licence usually stops being valid after you have been a registered resident in Sweden for more than one year.

There are exceptions and special cases, for example for licences from the United Kingdom and the Faroe Islands, and for some temporary stays. Do not assume that a residence permit or work permit automatically gives you an exemption.

Translation and ID

Some licences need a certified translation to be valid in Sweden. This can apply if the licence does not follow the relevant international road traffic conventions and is not written in English, German or French.

If the licence does not include a photograph, you may also need to carry valid photo identification. The safest habit is to keep your driving licence, ID and any required translation together when you drive.

Can you exchange a non-EEA licence?

Most non-EEA licences cannot simply be exchanged for a Swedish licence. Transportstyrelsen lists only certain exceptions, including licences from the United Kingdom, the Faroe Islands, Switzerland and Japan, under specific conditions.

If you need to keep driving without interruption, timing matters. In some exchange cases, the application should reach Transportstyrelsen within one year after you first register in Sweden.

When should you start the Swedish process?

If your licence will stop being valid after one year and cannot be exchanged, start the Swedish category B process early. Do not wait until the last month. You may need learner's permit, eye test, risk training, theory test and driving test.

Starting early gives you time to learn Swedish traffic rules, adapt to winter and city traffic, and book tests without panic.

Simple checklist

Check the issuing country, licence validity, registration date in Sweden, translation needs, photo ID needs and exchange options. If the rules point toward a Swedish licence, plan the process before your foreign licence expires in Sweden.

The key is not to guess. Confirm your exact situation with Transportstyrelsen and build your driving plan from there.
