Private driving practice in Sweden: handledare and introduction course

Private driving practice can make the Swedish category B process more flexible and less expensive. But it is not informal driving with a family member. In Sweden, private practice has clear rules, and both the learner and the supervisor need to be properly prepared.

The supervisor is called a handledare. The learner is called an elev. Together they can practise only when the legal requirements are in place.

What must be ready first?

The learner must have a valid learner's permit, called körkortstillstånd. If you are practising with a passenger car, both learner and supervisor must also complete a three-hour introduction course before private practice starts.

The supervisor must be approved by Transportstyrelsen. During practice, the supervisor should have the approval certificate with them. The green and white ÖVNINGSKÖR sign must be clearly visible at the back of the car.

The introduction course

The introduction course is often called handledarutbildning. It is the same course for the learner and the supervisor, but they do not have to attend at the same time.

The course is meant to help both people understand the goals of driver training, the rules for practice, how to plan lessons and how safety and the environment fit into learning to drive. It is valid for five years from the day it is completed.

What the supervisor is responsible for

During private practice, the supervisor is responsible and is treated as the driver. That means the practice should be planned carefully. The supervisor should choose suitable roads, avoid pressure, give clear instructions and stop the session if the learner is tired or the situation becomes too difficult.

Good private practice is calm, structured and repetitive. It is not about testing the learner in the hardest traffic as early as possible.

How to plan practice

Start in quiet areas. Practise starting, stopping, steering, mirrors, blind spots and positioning before moving to busier traffic. Then add roundabouts, lane changes, parking, country roads and city traffic step by step.

Keep short notes after each session: what went well, what felt unsafe and what to practise next. This makes private practice work better with driving school lessons.

Common mistakes

The first mistake is practising before all approvals are complete. The second is using private practice as random driving without a plan. The third is letting family stress enter the car.

Private practice works best when the supervisor stays patient and the learner gets clear, realistic tasks. If a situation feels too difficult, step back and train the same skill in an easier place.

Simple checklist

Before you drive privately, check: learner permit, introduction course, supervisor approval, approval certificate, ÖVNINGSKÖR sign and a realistic practice plan.

When these parts are in place, private practice can become one of the strongest parts of your Swedish driving education.

Practical quality checklist

This page is most useful when you connect it to real driving practice. For this topic, keep the main focus clear: A practical guide to private driving practice in Sweden: approved supervisor, introduction course, L-plate, documents and how to plan safe practice.

  • Before you start, name the exact risk you are training today and where it usually appears in Swedish traffic.
  • During practice, connect the theory rule to observation, speed, road position and communication with other road users.
  • After practice, write down one situation that felt late or uncertain and decide what earlier clue you should notice next time.
  • When reviewing practice questions, explain why the safe answer is safe instead of only remembering the correct option.
